SECTION 1

What Is an Option?

An option is a contract that gives you the right — but not the obligation — to buy or sell 100 shares of a stock at a specific price, before a specific date. You pay a small premium upfront for this right.

Think of it like a reservation. If you put a $50 deposit on a hotel room at a locked-in rate, you have the right to stay — but you're not forced to. If rates go up, your reservation is valuable. If rates drop, you let it expire and lose your deposit. Options work the same way.

The Two Types of Options

TYPEWHAT IT GIVES YOUYOU PROFIT WHEN...MAX LOSS
CALL OptionRight to BUY 100 shares at the strike priceStock price goes UP above your strikePremium paid (cost of the contract)
PUT OptionRight to SELL 100 shares at the strike priceStock price goes DOWN below your strikePremium paid (cost of the contract)

THE MOST IMPORTANT CONCEPT: OPTIONS DECAY EVERY DAY

Every single day you hold an option, it loses value from time decay (theta).

This is why BUYING options is hard — you need to be right about direction AND timing.

This is why SELLING options (covered calls, cash-secured puts) works in your favor — you collect the premium and let time decay work FOR you, not against you.

ShadowTape is built to help you SELL options in the right environment. That is the highest-probability strategy for a beginning trader.

SECTION 2

Your Two Starter Strategies

As a beginner focused on high success rate and small starting capital, you will use exactly two strategies. Both involve SELLING options — meaning you collect premium upfront and profit when the option expires worthless.

Strategy 1 — Covered Call

WHAT IT IS
  • You own 100 shares of a stock
  • You sell someone else the right to buy your shares at a higher price by a certain date
  • You collect premium immediately
  • If stock stays below that price: you keep the premium and your shares
  • If stock rises above that price: you sell your shares at the strike — still profitable

Example: AAPL at $188, you own 100 shares

  • Sell 1 covered call at the $195 strike expiring in 2 weeks
  • Collect $2.50 premium = $250 cash in your account immediately
  • If AAPL stays below $195: expires worthless, you keep $250 ✅
  • If AAPL rises above $195: shares sold at $195 — still profitable from $188 ✅
SCENARIOSTOCK AT EXPIRYYOUR RESULT
Best caseBelow $195Keep $250 premium + keep all 100 shares. Repeat next week.
Good caseAbove $195Shares called away at $195. Profit = ($195-$188)×100 + $250 = $950 total.
Bad caseDrops to $175Lost on stock but kept $250 premium — partially offsets loss.
🔵 SHADOWTAPE: WHEN TO SELL A COVERED CALL
  • Pressure Score ≥ 55 (neutral to bullish)
  • Positive GEX — dealers dampen moves, reducing risk of sharp spike through your strike
  • Sell strike AT or ABOVE the Call Wall shown in ShadowTape
  • Sell strike above the Gamma Flip level
  • Avoid if Pressure Score < 40 — stock may drop significantly

Strategy 2 — Cash-Secured Put (CSP)

WHAT IT IS
  • You sell someone the right to SELL you 100 shares at a lower price
  • You collect premium immediately and keep cash as collateral
  • If stock stays above your strike: option expires worthless, you keep the premium ✅
  • If stock drops below your strike: you buy 100 shares at that price
  • → This is fine if you want to own the stock at that price

Example: AAPL at $188

  • Sell 1 cash-secured put at the $180 strike expiring in 2 weeks
  • Keep $18,000 cash as collateral (100 × $180)
  • Collect $1.80 premium = $180 cash immediately
  • If AAPL stays above $180: expires worthless, keep $180 ✅
  • If AAPL drops to $175: you buy 100 shares at $180 (effective cost = $178.20) ✅
SCENARIOSTOCK AT EXPIRYYOUR RESULT
Best caseAbove $180Keep $180 premium. Never bought shares. Repeat next week.
Good case$175–$180Buy shares at $180. Effective cost $178.20. Own quality stock at discount.
Bad caseDrops to $160Own shares at $178.20. Unrealized loss, but kept premium.
🔵 SHADOWTAPE: WHEN TO SELL A CASH-SECURED PUT
  • Pressure Score ≥ 60 (bullish bias)
  • Stock price is ABOVE the Gamma Flip
  • Positive GEX — dealers buy dips, providing natural support
  • Sell strike AT or BELOW the Put Wall shown in ShadowTape
  • Only on stocks you genuinely want to own if assigned
  • NEVER sell CSPs before earnings — IV crush risk is severe

Step 2 — Selling a Cash-Secured Put

1

Log into Schwab → search your ticker → Trade → Options

2

Select expiration date (14–21 days out)

3

Find your target strike in the Put column

4

Click the BID price (selling = you receive the bid)

5

Order ticket: SELL TO OPEN · 1 contract · PUT · your strike · LIMIT at bid

6

Review → Place Order. Collateral is automatically held from your cash.

⚠ ALWAYS USE LIMIT ORDERS — NEVER MARKET ORDERS

Market orders on options can fill at terrible prices due to wide bid/ask spreads.

Set a limit at or near the current bid. If it doesn't fill in 5 minutes, adjust down $0.05 and retry.

Step 3 — Selling a Covered Call

You must already own 100 shares before selling a covered call.

1

Search ticker → Trade → Options → select expiration (7–21 days out)

3

Click BID → Order ticket: SELL TO OPEN · CALL · your strike · LIMIT

4

Review → Place Order

Using the Blue Bar Signal

When ShadowTape fires a Blue Bar signal, all three conditions are simultaneously true:

  • Call V/OI > 1.5 — institutions opening new bullish positions today
  • Price not extended (≤1.5% move) — pressure loading before the move
  • Positive GEX — dealers stabilize dips, supporting your trade

A Blue Bar is your highest-confidence entry. When one fires: sell a CSP at or below the Put Wall, 14–21 days out, limit order at bid. Stop: if price closes below Gamma Flip 2 days running, reassess.

HONEST LIMITATIONS — READ THIS
  • ShadowTape reads institutional OPTIONS flow — not stock direction guarantees
  • A Blue Bar means pressure is loading — it does NOT guarantee the stock will go up
  • GEX uses end-of-day open interest — intraday changes are not reflected
  • Dark Pool levels are estimated from high-OI clusters, not true dark pool data
  • Your edge comes from consistently trading in high-probability environments — not from predicting the market

The 5 Unbreakable Rules

RULE 1 — NEVER trade before earnings

Options premiums explode before earnings then collapse immediately after (IV crush). Check earnings dates on Schwab before every trade. No exceptions.

RULE 2 — NEVER use margin

Cash-secured puts require CASH collateral. Never borrow to fund them. Margin amplifies losses. You are building sustainable income, not gambling.

RULE 3 — NEVER sell options when SPY score is below 40

A bearish market means your puts are at high risk. Wait for the environment to improve. Missing a trade is free. Losses cost real money.

RULE 4 — Close at 50% profit — always

If you sold a put for $2.00, close it when it reaches $1.00. Taking 50% removes all risk and frees capital. The last 50% of profit takes 80% of the time and risk.

RULE 5 — If assigned, do not panic

If your put gets assigned, you now own 100 shares at your strike price. This is fine if you chose a quality stock. Immediately sell a covered call on your new shares. This is called the Wheel Strategy.

The Wheel Strategy

CONTINUOUS INCOME LOOP

STEP 1: Sell a cash-secured put below current price → collect premium

If expires worthless → repeat Step 1

If assigned → move to Step 2


STEP 2: You own 100 shares → sell a covered call above current price → collect premium

If expires worthless → repeat Step 2

If called away → move back to Step 1


Result: Continuously collecting premium whether the stock goes up, sideways, or slightly down.
